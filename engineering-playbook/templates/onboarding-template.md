## 🧭 General Philosophy
Our goal is not to have you shipping critical features on Day 1. Our goal is to ensure you understand our domain, our architecture, and our engineering standards so you can ship confidently on Day 90. Ask questions. Break things locally. 

---

## 🗓️ First 30 Days: Context & Culture
**Goal:** Understand the product, set up the environment, and merge a minor PR.

- [ ] **Day 1-3:** Environment Setup. Get Docker, DBs, and local servers running.
- [ ] **Day 4-7:** Product Deep Dive. Use the product as a customer. Read the API documentation.
- [ ] **Week 2:** Read the `engineering-playbook`. Understand our Git workflow and PR standards.
- [ ] **Week 3:** Pick up a "Good First Issue" (e.g., a small bug fix or test coverage improvement).
- [ ] **Week 4:** Successfully merge your first PR to production.

---

## 🗓️ 60 Days: Autonomy & Architecture
**Goal:** Take ownership of a medium-sized feature and understand the system architecture.

- [ ] **Week 5:** Pair program with a senior engineer on a complex system (e.g., Payout orchestration).
- [ ] **Week 6-7:** Take ownership of a standard feature ticket. Write the technical design document (RFC/ADR) for it.
- [ ] **Week 8:** Present your design at the weekly Engineering Sync. Lead the implementation.

---

## 🗓️ 90 Days: Impact & Scaling
**Goal:** Full autonomy and identifying technical debt.

- [ ] **Week 9-10:** Deliver your feature to production. Set up the Datadog monitoring and alerts for it.
- [ ] **Week 11:** Participate in the On-Call rotation shadow shift.
- [ ] **Week 12:** Identify one piece of technical debt or onboarding friction you encountered, and propose a fix for it.
