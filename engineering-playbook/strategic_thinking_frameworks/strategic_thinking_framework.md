# Strategic Thinking Framework and Template

This document provides a structured approach to strategic thinking. It synthesizes best practices from established strategy models (like Roger Martin's "Playing to Win," OGSM, and McKinsey's frameworks) into an actionable template.

---

## Part 1: The Strategic Thinking Framework

Strategic thinking is the process of analyzing the current environment, defining a future vision, and making deliberate choices about resource allocation to achieve a competitive advantage or specific goal.

A robust strategic thinking framework involves five core phases:

### 1. Situational Awareness (Where are we now?)
Understand the current reality without bias. This involves analyzing the internal capabilities and the external environment.
*   **External Analysis:** Market trends, competitor actions, regulatory changes, technological shifts (e.g., PESTLE analysis).
*   **Internal Analysis:** Strengths, weaknesses, current resource constraints, core competencies.
*   **The Core Problem/Opportunity:** Distill the analysis into a single, clear statement of the primary challenge or opportunity.

### 2. Strategic Ambition (Where do we want to be?)
Define the desired future state.
*   **Vision/Mission:** What is the ultimate purpose or North Star?
*   **Winning Aspiration:** What does "winning" look like in this specific context? (Not just playing, but winning).
*   **Objectives:** High-level, qualitative goals.

### 3. Strategic Choices (How will we win?)
Strategy is about making hard choices and trade-offs. If your strategy doesn't rule out otherwise good options, it's not a strategy.
*   **Where to Play:** Which markets, customer segments, channels, or product categories will we focus on? (And crucially, which will we *ignore*?)
*   **How to Win:** What is our unique value proposition? How will we differentiate ourselves in the chosen areas? (Cost leadership, differentiation, niche focus).

### 4. Capabilities and Systems (What do we need?)
Identify the engine that will drive the strategy.
*   **Core Capabilities:** What must we be uniquely good at to execute the "How to Win" choices?
*   **Management Systems:** What processes, structures, and metrics are needed to support these capabilities?

### 5. Execution and Adaptation (How do we act and learn?)
Translate the strategy into action and measure progress.
*   **Action Plan:** Short-term and medium-term initiatives.
*   **Metrics (OKRs/KPIs):** How will we measure success?
*   **Feedback Loops:** When and how will we review and pivot if the strategy isn't working?
