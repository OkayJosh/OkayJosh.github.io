## 1. SWOT Analysis (The Foundation)

**Best for:** A quick, structured brainstorm to understand where a business (or a specific initiative) currently stands before formulating a new strategy. It is the most universally recognized strategic tool.

### The Framework
SWOT divides analysis into internal factors (things you control) and external factors (things outside your control).

## 2. Kano Model (Feature Prioritization & Customer Delight)

**Best for:** Product strategy. Deciding which features to build next by understanding how different types of features impact customer satisfaction.

### The Framework
Not all features are created equal. The Kano model classifies product attributes into three main categories based on how they affect customer satisfaction over time:

1.  **Must-Be (Basic Needs):** If you don't have these, the customer is furious. If you *do* have them, the customer is neutral (they expect it). *Example: Brakes on a car.*
2.  **One-Dimensional (Performance Needs):** The better you execute these, the happier the customer is. It's a linear relationship. *Example: Gas mileage on a car.*
3.  **Attractive (Excitement Needs/Delighters):** The customer doesn't expect these. If you don't have them, they don't care. If you *do* have them, they are delighted. *Example: A car that parks itself.* (Note: Over time, Delighters become Must-Bes).

### Template: Feature Classification

| Proposed Feature | Kano Classification | Investment Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| e.g., Secure Login | **Must-Be** | Build to the industry standard. Do not over-invest; you won't get extra credit for it. |
| e.g., Search Speed | **Performance** | Invest heavily. Be objectively faster than the competition. |
| e.g., AI Auto-Summary | **Delighter** | Innovate here to create a USP (Unique Selling Proposition) and generate marketing buzz. |

## 3. Core Competence Model (Hamel & Prahalad)

**Best for:** Identifying the deep, underlying capabilities that allow a company to enter entirely new markets, rather than just looking at the products they currently sell.

### The Framework
A company should be viewed not as a portfolio of products, but as a portfolio of competencies. A "Core Competence" is a harmonized combination of multiple resources and skills that distinguish a firm in the marketplace.

To be a true Core Competence, it must pass three tests:
1.  **Relevance:** Does it provide significant value to the customer?
2.  **Difficulty of Imitation:** Is it difficult for competitors to copy?
3.  **Breadth of Application:** Does it provide potential access to a wide variety of markets?

*Example: Honda's core competence isn't "cars" or "motorcycles"—it's "small engine design and manufacturing," which allows them to easily enter markets for lawnmowers, generators, and outboard motors.*

## 4. The GE-McKinsey Nine-Box Matrix

**Best for:** Corporate strategy and portfolio management. It is a more sophisticated and nuanced alternative to the BCG Matrix (which only looks at growth and market share).

### The Framework
This matrix evaluates business units (or products) on two multi-factor axes to determine where to invest, hold, or divest.

1.  **Industry Attractiveness (Y-Axis):** High, Medium, Low. (Calculated by looking at market size, growth rate, profit margins, competitive intensity, etc.)
2.  **Business Unit Strength (X-Axis):** Strong, Average, Weak. (Calculated by looking at market share, brand equity, distribution channels, production capacity, etc.)

### Template: The Nine-Box Grid

*Plot your business units in the appropriate boxes.*

| | **Strong Business Unit** | **Average Business Unit** | **Weak Business Unit** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Industry Attractiveness** | **INVEST / GROW**<br>(Protect position, invest heavily) | **INVEST / GROW**<br>(Build to become strong) | **SELECTIVE INVESTMENT**<br>(Invest to fix weaknesses, or divest if unfixable) |
| **Medium Industry Attractiveness**| **INVEST / GROW**<br>(Build selectively) | **HOLD / SELECTIVE**<br>(Maintain status quo, generate cash) | **HARVEST / DIVEST**<br>(Take the cash, prepare to exit) |
| **Low Industry Attractiveness** | **HOLD / SELECTIVE**<br>(Protect in profitable niches) | **HARVEST / DIVEST**<br>(Manage for cash flow, no new investment) | **DIVEST**<br>(Exit the market immediately) |
