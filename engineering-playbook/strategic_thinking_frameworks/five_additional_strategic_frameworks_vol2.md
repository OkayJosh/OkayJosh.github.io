# Five Additional Strategic Thinking Frameworks (Volume 2)

This document outlines five more classic and highly effective strategic thinking frameworks. These are particularly useful for analyzing internal resources, evaluating growth opportunities, and managing a portfolio of products or initiatives.

---

## 1. VRIO Framework (Resource-Based View)

**Best for:** Evaluating whether your company's internal resources and capabilities can provide a sustained competitive advantage.

### The Framework
To determine if a resource or capability is a true competitive advantage, it must be evaluated against four criteria:
1.  **Value (V):** Does the resource enable the firm to exploit an opportunity or neutralize an external threat?
2.  **Rarity (R):** Is the resource currently controlled by only a small number of competing firms?
3.  **Imitability (I):** Do firms without the resource face a significant cost disadvantage in obtaining or developing it?
4.  **Organization (O):** Are the firm's other policies and procedures organized to support the exploitation of this resource?

### Template: VRIO Analysis

| Resource / Capability | Valuable? | Rare? | Costly to Imitate? | Organized to Capture? | Competitive Implications |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| e.g., Patented Tech | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes | **Sustained Competitive Advantage** |
| e.g., Brand Reputation | Yes | Yes | No | Yes | **Temporary Competitive Advantage** |
| e.g., Generic Software | Yes | No | No | Yes | **Competitive Parity** |
| e.g., Outdated Machinery | No | No | No | No | **Competitive Disadvantage** |

## 2. Ansoff Matrix (Growth Strategy)

**Best for:** Deciding how to grow the business by evaluating the risks associated with different product/market combinations.

### The Framework
The Ansoff Matrix plots growth strategies based on whether you are selling existing or new products into existing or new markets. The risk increases as you move away from existing products and markets.

### Template: The 2x2 Growth Matrix

| | **Existing Products** | **New Products** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Existing Markets** | **1. Market Penetration (Lowest Risk)**<br>*Strategy:* Sell more of what we have to our current target audience.<br>*Tactics:* Promotions, volume discounts, aggressive marketing. | **2. Product Development (Medium Risk)**<br>*Strategy:* Develop new products to sell to our existing customer base.<br>*Tactics:* R&D, brand extensions, new features. |
| **New Markets** | **3. Market Development (Medium Risk)**<br>*Strategy:* Take our existing products into new demographics or geographies.<br>*Tactics:* International expansion, targeting different age groups. | **4. Diversification (Highest Risk)**<br>*Strategy:* Create entirely new products for entirely new markets.<br>*Tactics:* Mergers & Acquisitions, incubating new startups internally. |

## 3. Porter's Five Forces (Industry Analysis)

**Best for:** Understanding the competitive intensity and overall attractiveness (profitability) of an industry before entering it or when assessing a major strategic shift.

### The Framework
Profitability in an industry is dictated by five forces. The stronger the forces, the lower the profit potential.

### Template: Five Forces Assessment
*Rate each force from Low to High and document the reasoning.*

1.  **Threat of New Entrants (Barriers to Entry): [Low/Med/High]**
    *   *Analysis:* How easy is it for a new startup to enter this market? (Consider capital requirements, patents, brand loyalty).
2.  **Bargaining Power of Suppliers: [Low/Med/High]**
    *   *Analysis:* Can our suppliers easily drive up prices? Are there many suppliers or only a few monopolies?
3.  **Bargaining Power of Buyers (Customers): [Low/Med/High]**
    *   *Analysis:* Can our customers easily drive down prices? Are they highly concentrated? Is it easy for them to switch to a competitor?
4.  **Threat of Substitute Products: [Low/Med/High]**
    *   *Analysis:* Can customers solve their problem using a completely different method? (e.g., Video conferencing vs. Business travel).
5.  **Rivalry Among Existing Competitors: [Low/Med/High]**
    *   *Analysis:* Is the competition cutthroat (competing purely on price) or do firms respect different niches?

## 4. The Balanced Scorecard (Strategic Performance Management)

**Best for:** Ensuring that you are tracking holistic organizational health, not just lagging financial metrics, when executing a strategy.

### The Framework
The Balanced Scorecard forces leaders to measure performance across four distinct perspectives, linking daily operations to long-term strategy.

### Template: The Scorecard

| Perspective | Strategic Objective | Key Performance Indicator (KPI) | Target | Current Status |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Financial**<br>*(How do we look to shareholders?)* | e.g., Increase profitability | e.g., Net Profit Margin | 15% | 12% |
| **2. Customer**<br>*(How do customers see us?)* | e.g., Improve customer loyalty | e.g., Net Promoter Score (NPS) | 60 | 45 |
| **3. Internal Processes**<br>*(What must we excel at?)* | e.g., Accelerate time-to-market | e.g., Concept-to-launch time | < 3 months | 5 months |
| **4. Learning & Growth**<br>*(How can we continue to improve?)* | e.g., Upskill engineering team | e.g., % of team certified in AI | 50% | 10% |

## 5. BCG Matrix (Portfolio Management)

**Best for:** Large companies deciding where to allocate capital across multiple different product lines or business units.

### The Framework
The Boston Consulting Group (BCG) Matrix plots business units based on their Market Growth Rate and Relative Market Share.

### Template: The Portfolio Map

| | **High Market Share** | **Low Market Share** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Market Growth** | **STARS**<br>*Profile:* High growth, high share. They need heavy investment to sustain growth but generate strong cash.<br>*Strategy:* Invest aggressively to maintain market leadership. | **QUESTION MARKS**<br>*Profile:* High growth, low share. They consume a lot of cash but bring little in return.<br>*Strategy:* Analyze heavily. Either invest heavily to turn them into Stars, or divest/kill them. |
| **Low Market Growth** | **CASH COWS**<br>*Profile:* Low growth, high share. The mature, foundational products of the company.<br>*Strategy:* "Milk" them. Minimize investment and use the cash generated to fund Stars and promising Question Marks. | **DOGS**<br>*Profile:* Low growth, low share. They barely break even.<br>*Strategy:* Divest, liquidate, or pivot. Do not invest capital here. |
