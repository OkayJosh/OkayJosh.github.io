# Five Additional Strategic Thinking Frameworks

This document outlines five distinct strategic thinking frameworks, each offering a unique lens for analyzing situations, making choices, and planning for the future.

---

## 1. Wardley Mapping (Value Chain Evolution)

**Best for:** Understanding the competitive landscape, identifying where to innovate, and deciding what to build, buy, or outsource.

### The Framework
Wardley Mapping visualizes a business landscape by plotting components based on two axes:
1.  **Value Chain (y-axis):** User needs at the top, and the underlying components needed to satisfy them below (invisible to the user).
2.  **Evolution (x-axis):** How mature a component is, moving from Genesis (brand new) -> Custom Built -> Product (+Rental) -> Commodity (+Utility).

**The Core Idea:** Strategy is fluid. Components constantly evolve towards commodity due to competition. You must map your landscape to understand where you are playing and anticipate where the market is moving.

### Template / Steps to Execute
1.  **Identify the Anchor:** Who is the user and what is their core need?
2.  **Map the Value Chain:** What components are required to meet that need? (e.g., Application -> Database -> Compute -> Power).
3.  **Plot the Evolution:** For each component, determine its evolutionary stage (Genesis to Commodity).
4.  **Analyze & Act:**
    *   *Where are we custom-building something that should be a commodity (wasted effort)?*
    *   *Where is a component evolving, enabling new higher-level innovations?*
    *   *Action:* Decide to Outsource/Use Utility for commodities, and focus internal engineering on Genesis/Custom Built areas that provide competitive differentiation.

## 2. Blue Ocean Strategy (Value Innovation)

**Best for:** Escaping cutthroat competition (Red Oceans) by creating new, uncontested market space (Blue Oceans).

### The Framework
Instead of fighting competitors over the same customers, Blue Ocean Strategy focuses on "Value Innovation"—simultaneously pursuing differentiation and low cost to create a new market.

### Template: The ERRC Grid (Eliminate-Reduce-Raise-Create)
To craft a Blue Ocean strategy, answer these four questions about your industry's standard practices:

| Action | Strategic Question | Your Plan |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Eliminate** | Which factors that the industry has long competed on should be eliminated entirely? (e.g., Cirque du Soleil eliminated animal acts). | [List factors to eliminate] |
| **Reduce** | Which factors should be reduced well below the industry's standard? | [List factors to reduce] |
| **Raise** | Which factors should be raised well above the industry's standard? | [List factors to raise] |
| **Create** | Which factors should be created that the industry has never offered? | [List factors to create] |

## 3. McKinsey's Three Horizons of Growth

**Best for:** Balancing short-term performance with long-term innovation and survival. Managing an investment portfolio.

### The Framework
A company must simultaneously manage three horizons to ensure continuous growth:
*   **Horizon 1 (H1) - Core Business:** Defend and extend current core businesses. Focus on margin improvement, efficiency, and continuous innovation.
*   **Horizon 2 (H2) - Emerging Opportunities:** Build emerging businesses. Take proven concepts and scale them to generate substantial new revenue streams.
*   **Horizon 3 (H3) - Future bets:** Create options for future businesses. Exploratory research, pilot projects, and disruptive ideas that might be the core business in 5-10 years.

### Template: Portfolio Allocation
*Map your current strategic initiatives across the three horizons.*

| Horizon | Focus | Target Allocation (e.g., 70/20/10) | Current Initiatives | Success Metric |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Horizon 1** | Defend & Extend Core | [e.g., 70% of resources] | 1. [Initiative]<br>2. [Initiative] | Profit, ROI, Market Share |
| **Horizon 2** | Build Emerging | [e.g., 20% of resources] | 1. [Initiative] | Revenue Growth, Market Penetration |
| **Horizon 3** | Create Future Options | [e.g., 10% of resources] | 1. [Initiative] | Milestones met, Learning, Prototypes |

## 4. Scenario Planning

**Best for:** Navigating high uncertainty, long-term planning, and ensuring resilience against unpredictable future events.

### The Framework
Instead of predicting *the* future, scenario planning involves creating multiple plausible futures and testing strategies against all of them.

### Template: The 2x2 Scenario Matrix
1.  **Identify Driving Forces:** What are the biggest macro trends affecting your industry? (e.g., AI adoption rate, regulatory changes, economic climate).
2.  **Identify Critical Uncertainties:** Pick the two forces that are both *highly impactful* and *highly uncertain*.
3.  **Create the Matrix:** Put one uncertainty on the X-axis and one on the Y-axis. This creates 4 quadrants (scenarios).

| Scenario 1 (Top Left) | Scenario 2 (Top Right) |
| :--- | :--- |
| *[High Uncertainty A]*<br>*[Low Uncertainty B]*<br>**Narrative:** [Brief story of this world] | *[High Uncertainty A]*<br>*[High Uncertainty B]*<br>**Narrative:** [Brief story of this world] |
| **Scenario 3 (Bottom Left)** | **Scenario 4 (Bottom Right)** |
| *[Low Uncertainty A]*<br>*[Low Uncertainty B]*<br>**Narrative:** [Brief story of this world] | *[Low Uncertainty A]*<br>*[High Uncertainty B]*<br>**Narrative:** [Brief story of this world] |

4.  **Develop Strategies:** What strategies work well in *all* scenarios? (Robust strategies). What strategies are specific to one scenario? (Contingency plans).

## 5. Objectives and Key Results (OKRs)

**Best for:** Bridging the gap between high-level strategy and operational execution. Aligning teams.

### The Framework
While the frameworks above help *formulate* strategy, OKRs help *execute* it.
*   **Objectives (Where do we want to go?):** Qualitative, inspirational, time-bound goals.
*   **Key Results (How will we know we are getting there?):** Quantitative, measurable outcomes that indicate progress toward the objective.

### Template
**Objective 1:** [Inspirational, qualitative goal. e.g., "Become the most loved productivity tool for engineers."]
*   **Key Result 1:** [Measurable outcome. e.g., "Increase Net Promoter Score (NPS) from 40 to 60."]
*   **Key Result 2:** [Measurable outcome. e.g., "Achieve a 90% weekly active user (WAU) retention rate."]
*   **Key Result 3:** [Measurable outcome. e.g., "Reduce average user onboarding time to under 3 minutes."]

*   **Initiatives (Output):** [What tasks will we do to drive these results? e.g., "Redesign onboarding flow", "Implement in-app feedback tool."]
